Biography

Who is Cesar Pelli?

Cesar Pelli is a renowned Argentinian architect who has left an indelible mark on architecture around the world. His distinctive creativity and talent for blending modernity and context have resulted in iconic works that redefine our vision of urban spaces.

Overview of the exhibition

Petronas Towers (Kuala Lumpur - MY) • 1998

Emblematic of the city of Kuala Lumpur, these twin towers have single-handedly defined the Malaysian skyline. At the time of their completion, they were the tallest towers in the world, surpassed only by the Taipei 101 in 2004, which was itself surpassed by the Burj Khalifa in 2010.

World Financial Center (New York - US) • 1985-88

Office complex located in Battery Park City in Manhattan, comprising several towers in the post-modern style.

Sunset Mountain Park Urban Nucleus (Los Angeles, Santa Monica Mountains - US) • 1964-66

Combining contemporary architecture and nature. It's a dynamic space where elegant forms and functionality could have blended harmoniously together.

COMSAT Laboratories (Clarksburg, Maryland - US) • 1969

As a research and development center, this facility is a successful blend of modern aesthetics and functionality. The building's distinctive forms reflect an avant-garde approach, creating a place of sophistication at the heart of the technological landscape.

Pacific Design Center (West Hollywood - US) • 1975-2010

A bold, colourful landmark. These three brightly coloured towers house spaces dedicated to design, fashion and the visual arts. And that's not counting the fact that this space also adds a distinctive artistic touch to the surrounding urban environment.

Sevilla Tower (Seville - ES) • 2016

Standing elegantly and enhancing the low-lying city skyline. Emblematic, it testifies to the architect's talent for blending contemporary aesthetics with fluid lines, adding a distinctive silhouette to the city skyline.

National Museum of Art (Osaka - JP) • 2004

A harmonious blend of culture and architecture. The modern lines and imposing presence of the building evoke a successful fusion between a living creature and something mechanical, making the museum an architectural gem in the very heart of the Japanese city of Osaka.

Weber Music Hall (Duluth, Minnesota - US) • 2005

A distinctive creation combining architectural ingenuity and functionality, the building offers a space dedicated to music, where modernity meets artistic expression. The boldness of the design is reflected in a structure that blends seamlessly into its environment.
