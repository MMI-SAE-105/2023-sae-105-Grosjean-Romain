Petronas Towers

(Kuala Lumpur - MY) • 1998

Touching the Sky...

As an internationally recognised landmark, the Petronas Twin Towers symbolise the courage, ingenuity, initiative, and determination, energy, confidence, optimism, advancement and zest of a nation.

Architectural Style and Influence

The Petronas Twin Towers, designed by renowned architect César Antonio Pelli, embodies modernism with its clean aesthetic, use of contemporary materials such as glass and steel, and focus on functionality.

Iconic symbols

Pelli, widely acclaimed for his contributions to the design of iconic skyscrapers, created the Petronas Twin Towers, which redefined the Kuala Lumpur skyline. From 1998 to 2004, they stood as the world's tallest structures, a testament to architectural daring and innovation.

Innovation in Design

The Twin Towers reflect Pelli's innovative approach to architecture. They incorporate contemporary elements while respecting the environment, marking a milestone in global architectural design.

Legacy

The Petronas Twin Towers carry with them the exceptional legacy of César Pelli. Their iconic silhouette, combined with the architect's innovation and creativity, continues to inspire professionals around the world. Pelli's impact on the architectural landscape remains undeniable, leaving behind a legacy that transcends geographical boundaries.
